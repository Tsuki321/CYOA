# Mages CYOA - Content Overhaul Style Guide

## Core Principles

### Tone & Voice
- **Grounded magitech sci-fi** - Think Lyrical Nanoha, not D&D fantasy
- **Technical precision** - Use specific numbers, measurable effects, device processing limits
- **No absolute language** - Avoid "perfect," "infinite," "absolute," "omnipotent," "flawless"
- **Bounded power** - Every capability has limits defined by mana throughput, device processing, or physics
- **Show, don't tell** - Use concrete examples instead of vague descriptions

### Spell Naming Convention
- **Avoid generic fantasy terms** - No "Fireball," "Ice Shard," "Lightning Bolt"
- **Use technical/tactical nomenclature** - Think military designations, scientific terms, or evocative compound words
- **Examples of good names:**
  - Instead of "Fireball" → "Thermal Lance" or "Plasma Sphere"
  - Instead of "Mind Shield" → "Cognitive Fortress" or "Neural Barrier"
  - Instead of "Teleport" → "Spatial Displacement" or "Phase Shift"

### Spell Proficiency Structure
Each proficiency level (beginner → apprentice → adept → expert → master) must show:

1. **Paragraph 1** (2-3 sentences): Core mechanics - how the device constructs the spell, mana cost, cast time
2. **Paragraph 2** (3-4 sentences): Performance metrics - range, power output, limitations, number of casts per cycle
3. **Paragraph 3** (2-3 sentences): Tactical application - what the spell does in combat/utility, ceiling and boundaries

**Progression across levels:**
- Beginner: Basic functionality, short range, high cost, slow cast, clear limitations
- Apprentice: Improved metrics, 2x range, better efficiency, tactical viability
- Adept: Professional-grade, remote features, 4x range, reliable in combat
- Expert: Advanced features, multi-target, 6-8x range, sustained use viable
- Master: Theoretical optimum for that tier, max efficiency, reflexive casting, but still bounded by tier limits

### Mana Reactor Ranks

Each rank must clearly show:
1. **Capacity increase** - How much more mana vs. previous rank (use multipliers)
2. **Recovery speed** - How fast ambient absorption works
3. **Unique capability** - What this rank enables that lower ranks cannot do
4. **Tactical impact** - What difference this makes in combat/operations
5. **Limitations/vulnerabilities** - What can still go wrong at this rank

**Power scaling:**
- Malkuth (10 PP): 1x baseline, ~2-3 casts per cycle, minutes to recover
- Yesod (20 PP): 2x capacity, ~5-6 casts per cycle, under 1 minute recovery
- Hod (30 PP): 3.5x capacity, ~8-10 casts per cycle, supports 4 concurrent effects
- Netzach (40 PP): 6x capacity, 3x endurance, recirculation extends combat time dramatically
- Tiferet (50 PP): 10x capacity, harmonic sync eliminates friction, passive mana gain
- Geburah (60 PP): 15x capacity, 2-3x damage multiplier through compression, stress on core
- Chesed (70 PP): 15x capacity, dispersion for healing/support, can share mana with allies
- Binah (80 PP): 25x capacity, real-time mana flow analysis, predictive combat capabilities
- Chokhmah (90 PP): 40x capacity, dynamic reconfiguration, improvised spell construction
- Keter (100 PP): 70x capacity, all-mode integration, anticipatory casting, practical ceiling

### Body/Device Perks

Must directly reference the percentage improvements:
- Tier 1: 10% improvement
- Tier 2: 25% improvement  
- Tier 3: 50% improvement
- Tier 4: 75% improvement
- Tier 5: 100% improvement (doubled)
- Tier 6: 200% improvement (tripled)
- Tier 7: 400% improvement (5x)
- Tier 8: 900% improvement (10x)
- Tier 9: 1900% improvement (20x)
- Tier 10: Practical ceiling (further investment yields diminishing returns)

Each tier description format:
1. Open with the multiplier/percentage: "Tier X: [Multiplier] enhancement..."
2. Provide 2-3 concrete, measurable examples with numbers
3. Explain tactical/practical impact
4. For Tier 10: Explicitly state this is the ceiling and why

### Class Adaptations

For the 1,425 class-specific proficiency entries:
- **Paragraph 1**: Maintain spell mechanics, swap class-specific nouns (blade/cannon/grimoire, melee/ranged/support)
- **Paragraph 2**: Completely unique per class, showing tactical role:
  - Knight: Close-quarters, blade-enhanced, mobility-focused
  - Bombardment: Long-range, artillery-style, area control
  - Thaumaturge: Support/healing, formation anchoring, buff/debuff focus

## Spell Tier Guidelines

- **Tier 1**: Basic combat utility - room-scale range, single-target damage comparable to conventional weapons
- **Tier 2**: Enhanced combat - building-scale range, multiple targets, improved damage
- **Tier 3**: Professional combat - city-block scale, significant area effects
- **Tier 4-5**: Elite operations - multi-block range, vehicle-destroying power
- **Tier 6-7**: Strategic assets - district-scale effects, structure-destroying capability
- **Tier 8-9**: Major strategic weapons - city-scale range, mass destruction potential
- **Tier 10**: Theoretical WMD tier - continental implications, reality-warping potential

## Forbidden Phrases

Avoid these unless specifically justified:
- "perfect," "flawless," "absolute," "infinite," "unlimited"
- "omnipotent," "omniscient," "godlike" (except as metaphor with clear limits)
- "impossible to defeat," "unstoppable," "invincible"
- "instantly," "immediately" (use "reflexive," "sub-second," specific timings instead)
- "forever," "eternal," "permanent" (unless magically bound with stated duration)

## Required Elements

Every spell proficiency must include:
- Specific range in meters
- Cast time in seconds (or "reflexive" for master tier)
- Number of casts per mana cycle for each reactor tier mentioned
- Device processing requirements/limitations
- Environmental factors that affect performance
- Clear ceiling statement at master tier

Every reactor rank must include:
- Capacity multiplier vs. Malkuth baseline
- Recovery time from full depletion
- Max concurrent spell effects sustainable
- Highest spell tier castable without mana bleed
- Failure mode or vulnerability

Every perk tier must include:
- The percentage/multiplier stated explicitly
- 2-3 concrete measurable examples
- Combat/practical application
- Tier 10 must state "practical ceiling" and "diminishing returns"
