## Key Themes

### Authority Structure
- Answerable to President, but can intervene against presidential abuse
- Effectively a fourth branch of government for magical matters
- First Seat has final authority (requires unanimous override)
- Pragmatic: "No conventional authority can compel a Keter-rank mage"

### Meritocracy
- Seats earned through capability, not political appointment
- Diverse specializations cover all aspects of magical governance
- International representation reflects global nature of magical threat/opportunity

### Power Justification
- Council exists because conventional authority cannot contain them
- Their existence is both protection and warning
- They enforce laws they create using devices they approve
- "Foundation upon which the entire magical world rests"
